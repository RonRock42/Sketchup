The role of images in emails

Many email clients (Outlook) don’t display images by default, others block images because of a personal preference. In only about half of the email opens the images show automatically. The other half of the recipients will initially not understand what an image-based email is a about. 

Emails that rely on images to communicate the main message should thus clearly be avoided. Hold on now, that does not mean they should not be used in email communication at all. 
Don’t assume recipients will automatically see the graphics in your email.
 

Emails that rely on images to communicate the main message should thus clearly be avoided. Hold on now, that does not mean they should not be used in email communication at all. Visuals are great to enhance the experience for the people that can, or decide, to view them. But don’t count on them to do the full job in telling the story.
 
Related…
 
 

Emails like need visible buttons

Congrats! You did a great job in getting the reader excited about the product, purpose or plan featured in that email. Now he or she wants to, no no, needs to act right away. Except, how? Where’s that fat button that takes them to the promissed land? A really fancy image was specially crafted to be that button, but it did not show up. Ooops…

The take away: don’t use images for key actions, use HTML. The HTML-based buttons will always show and work. With background and border controls they are easy to customize and will even look decent in Outlook. For the fancy pants email clients RED offers gradients and shadows too.
